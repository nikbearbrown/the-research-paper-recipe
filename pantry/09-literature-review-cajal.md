## Figure 1: Summary versus synthesis
Priority: Important
Trigger: VG — the difference between listing sources and extracting a cross-source pattern is a structural relationship prose names but cannot show
Figure type: Comparison panels
Concept statement: Summary lists what each source said as separate parallel statements; synthesis draws lines between sources to extract a shared pattern and locate the gap none of them fills.
Reader prior knowledge: Believes citing several sources in sequence constitutes a literature review.
Source anchor: Section: Let me make the distinction between summary and synthesis concrete.

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Build two panels side by side, each framed by a thin light gray border. Left panel (summary): three separate small rounded source nodes stacked vertically with no connections between them, each node drawn as a blank gray outline, isolated. Right panel (synthesis): the same three source nodes but now drawn in blue (#56B4E9) and joined by thin single-headed connector lines that converge on a single new node at the right — the cross-source claim — drawn in green (#009E73); below the converged claim node leave a small empty dashed-outline box representing the gap. Use neutral gray (#000000 thin) for the panel frames and connectors. Keep the source nodes identical in position across both panels so the only difference is the presence of connections and the emergent claim. Keep components to six: three source nodes, one claim node, one gap box, connectors as a set. Render as a blank unannotated flat vector on white, no text, 1pt strokes, single-headed arrows.

## Figure 2: The synthesis matrix
Priority: Critical
Trigger: VG — the matrix structure that makes gaps visible as empty cells is itself a spatial schematic, not depictable from prose
Figure type: Structural schematic
Concept statement: A theme-by-source matrix forces active reading and makes patterns and gaps visible — an empty region in the grid is precisely the unfilled space your study can occupy.
Reader prior knowledge: Reads sources one at a time and waits for the gap to occur to them.
Source anchor: Section: The tool I find most useful for building synthesis is a matrix.

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw a clean grid of six rows by five columns, all cell borders as thin black (#000000) 1pt lines, with a slightly heavier left column and top row to indicate the row-header and column-header bands (left for themes, top for sources). Fill most interior cells with a small neutral gray filled square or short dash to indicate "content present." Leave one contiguous block of cells — for example the cells where the bottom two rows meet the rightmost two columns — completely empty and outline that empty region with a single thin blue (#0072B2) rectangle to mark it as the visible gap. Keep the header bands unfilled. Keep components to: the grid, the header bands, the filled-cell marks (as one set), and the one highlighted empty-gap rectangle. Render as a blank unannotated flat vector on white, no text, no row or column names, no numerals, crisp aligned strokes.

## Figure 3: CARS three-move funnel
Priority: Critical
Trigger: MC — the introduction is a three-step sequence (establish territory → establish niche → occupy niche) where each step depends on the prior, and the funnel narrowing is the load-bearing structure
Figure type: Process flowchart
Concept statement: A research introduction moves through three dependent stages — establish the territory (broad), establish the niche (narrow to the gap), occupy the niche (the study) — narrowing like a funnel toward the hypothesis.
Reader prior knowledge: Writes introductions as literature parades without the niche pivot.
Source anchor: Section: He called it the Create a Research Space model — CARS.

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw an inverted-funnel of three stacked horizontal bands, widest at the top and narrowing downward, arranged vertically and connected by single-headed downward arrows between bands. The top band is the widest, drawn in neutral blue outline (#56B4E9) representing Move 1, establish the territory. The middle band is narrower, drawn in orange (#E69F00) representing Move 2, establish the niche — the pivot. The bottom band is the narrowest, drawn in green (#009E73) representing Move 3, occupy the niche, and below it a small downward arrow points to a single dot representing the arriving hypothesis. To the side of the middle (niche) band, draw a small empty dashed box with a thin connector — a reserved slot to later show "what happens when Move 2 is missing." Use thin black (#000000) arrows. Keep components to six: three bands, two connecting arrows, the hypothesis dot (the missing-niche slot counts within the niche band). Render as a blank unannotated flat vector on white, no text, 1pt strokes, single-headed arrows.

### Block 2 — Full SCOPE Prompt
[S - Specification] Single-column 89mm textbook, 300 DPI, vector, white, unannotated.
[C - Content] A three-band vertical funnel narrowing top to bottom — Move 1 territory (widest), Move 2 niche (middle pivot), Move 3 occupation (narrowest) — connected by downward single-headed arrows, ending in a hypothesis dot; a small dashed reserved slot beside the niche band.
[O - Organization] Stacked top-to-bottom, each band narrower than the one above; arrows flow downward; hypothesis dot below the bottom band.
[P - Presentation] Flat vector, Okabe-Ito — arrows #000000, Move 1 band #56B4E9 (primary/broad), Move 2 niche band #E69F00 (secondary/pivot), Move 3 band #009E73 (active/occupation), hypothesis dot #000000, 1pt strokes, single-headed arrows, no text in image.

## Figure 4: What AI can and cannot do in literature review
Priority: Important
Trigger: VG — the boundary between AI-appropriate support tasks and human-required verification tasks is a structural partition prose lists but cannot show
Figure type: Conceptual map
Concept statement: AI can cluster verified notes, suggest search terms, and diagnose source-by-source structure, but generating citations, summarizing unread papers, deciding the gap, and judging evidence quality must remain human — a sharp boundary, not a gradient.
Reader prior knowledge: May assume AI assistance and human verification blend into one another.
Source anchor: Section: Now a hard rule, because it matters and AI tools make it easy to violate.

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Divide the frame into two clearly separated regions by a single thick vertical black (#000000) dividing line down the center. On the left region, place a column of three or four blank rounded nodes outlined in green (#009E73) representing AI-appropriate support tasks. On the right region, place a column of three or four blank rounded nodes outlined in orange-red (#D55E00) representing human-required verification tasks. Make the central dividing line emphatic and unbroken to signal that the boundary is sharp, not a gradient — no arrows should cross it. Optionally place one small neutral icon at the top of each column (a generic gear-like glyph left, a generic eye/check glyph right), drawn schematically. Keep components to: the dividing line, the left node column, the right node column, two header glyphs. Render as a blank unannotated flat vector on white, no text, 1pt strokes, no crossing connectors.
