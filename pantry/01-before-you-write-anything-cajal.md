## Figure 1: Falsifiability Ladder
Priority: Critical
Trigger: VG — hierarchy/progression claim (vague → falsifiable) not depictable from prose alone
Figure type: Hierarchy/progression (vertical ladder)
Concept statement: A claim gains intellectual content as it is compressed from a vague topic into a falsifiable hypothesis that specifies what would count against it.
Reader prior knowledge: Reader knows the words "topic" and "hypothesis" loosely but has not seen the progression rendered as discrete rungs that each rule something out.
Source anchor: Section: Popper falsifiability / "what would have to be true for your paper to be wrong"

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw a vertical ladder of four stacked horizontal rungs ascending from bottom to top inside a single-column textbook frame on white. The bottom rung is the widest and lightest, each rung above it narrower, conveying compression as the claim climbs. Rung 1 (bottom, light gray) = vague topic; rung 2 (sky blue #56B4E9) = narrowed claim; rung 3 (blue #0072B2) = specified comparison; rung 4 (top, bluish green #009E73) = falsifiable hypothesis. To the right of each rung place a small open bracket-shaped callout marker (no text) pointing outward, representing "what this rung rules out," growing larger toward the top. Connect rungs with a single straight upward arrow on the left spine running bottom to top, indicating increasing specificity. Use 1pt strokes, flat fills, no shadows, no gradient. Keep every shape blank and unannotated — no text, numbers, or labels baked in. Components: four rungs, four rule-out markers, one ascending spine arrow. White background, generous margins, all shapes aligned on a shared left edge.

### Block 2 — Full SCOPE Prompt
[S - Specification] single-column 89mm textbook width, 300 DPI, vector, white background, unannotated.
[C - Content] four stacked rungs (vague topic → narrowed claim → specified comparison → falsifiable hypothesis), four "rules-out" marker brackets growing larger upward, one left-spine ascending arrow. Relationship to preserve: ascending specificity narrows the rung and enlarges what it rules out.
[O - Organization] vertical ladder, widest rung at bottom, narrowing upward; rule-out markers on the right; single arrow on the left spine; all rungs share a left alignment edge.
[P - Presentation] flat vector, Okabe-Ito: light gray (#999999) bottom rung, #56B4E9, #0072B2, #009E73 ascending; 1pt strokes; no text in image.
[E - Exclusions] no text, no numerals, no captions, no ladder side-rail wood texture, no human figures, no 3D, no shadows, no gradients.

### Block 3 — Negative Prompt
realistic ladder textures, wood grain, perspective rungs, text labels, words, gibberish letters, titles, captions, decorative borders, realistic textures, plastic wrap effects, drop shadows, gradient backgrounds, photographic elements, non-standard arrows, dual-headed arrows, hand-drawn styles, sketch lines, human figures, visual clutter, overlapping unaligned paths, fuzzy borders, watermarks, red-green color combinations, rainbow color scales, 3D perspective distortion

## Figure 2: Topic / Question / Hypothesis Comparison
Priority: Critical
Trigger: VG — structural three-way comparison of definitional categories not self-evident from running prose
Figure type: Comparison panels (three vertical columns)
Concept statement: A topic has no edges, a research question can be answered in principle, and a hypothesis is a falsifiable prediction with a mechanism — three distinct objects writers routinely conflate.
Reader prior knowledge: Reader has encountered all three terms but treats them as interchangeable stages rather than categorically different objects with different truth-status.
Source anchor: Section: "a topic, a research question, and a hypothesis"

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Lay out three equal-width vertical panels side by side inside a single-column textbook frame on white, separated by thin vertical rules. Panel 1 (light gray header band) = topic; panel 2 (sky blue #56B4E9 header band) = research question; panel 3 (bluish green #009E73 header band) = hypothesis. Within each panel stack three blank horizontal slots of equal height beneath the header, representing the comparison rows (definition / what evidence would look like / can it be false). In the bottom slot of each panel place a small status icon: panel 1 a hollow circle (cannot be false), panel 2 a half-filled circle (answerable in principle), panel 3 a solid filled circle (can be false). A single horizontal arrow runs left to right across the top spanning all three panels, indicating progression from topic to hypothesis. Use 1pt strokes, flat fills, no shadows or gradients. Every slot, header band, and icon must be blank and unannotated — no text or numbers baked in. Components: three panels, three header bands, nine row slots, three status icons, one progression arrow. White background, aligned baselines across panels.

### Block 2 — Full SCOPE Prompt
[S - Specification] single-column 89mm textbook, 300 DPI, vector, white, unannotated.
[C - Content] three panels (topic, research question, hypothesis), each with a colored header band and three blank stacked row slots, plus a status icon (hollow / half / solid circle) in the bottom slot; one left-to-right progression arrow across the top. Relationship to preserve: increasing falsifiability across the three categories.
[O - Organization] three equal columns separated by thin vertical rules; shared baselines across rows; progression arrow spanning the top.
[P - Presentation] flat vector, Okabe-Ito: gray (#999999), #56B4E9, #009E73 header bands; black strokes; 1pt; no text in image.
[E - Exclusions] no text, no row labels, no captions, no shading gradients, no icons beyond the three circles, no human figures, no 3D.

### Block 3 — Negative Prompt
filled spreadsheet cells with text, written words, numbers, titles, captions, decorative borders, realistic textures, plastic wrap effects, drop shadows, gradient backgrounds, photographic elements, non-standard arrows, dual-headed arrows, hand-drawn styles, sketch lines, human figures, visual clutter, overlapping unaligned paths, fuzzy borders, watermarks, red-green color combinations, rainbow color scales, 3D perspective distortion

## Figure 3: Rival Hypothesis Tree
Priority: Important
Trigger: MC — branching of one observation into four competing mechanisms, each implying a distinguishing design (≥3 interdependent elements)
Figure type: Hierarchy/taxonomy (single-root branching tree)
Concept statement: One observed result ("AI tutoring improves retention") can be produced by several distinct mechanisms, and the research design must be chosen to distinguish them.
Reader prior knowledge: Reader understands "alternative explanation" verbally but has not seen the fan-out of one surface result into mutually competing causal stories.
Source anchor: Section: rival hypotheses / Maya's four competing mechanisms

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw a top-rooted branching tree inside a single-column textbook frame on white. A single rounded root node sits centered at the top (sky blue #56B4E9), representing the shared observation. From it, four straight branch lines fan downward to four equally sized rounded child nodes in a horizontal row, each a competing mechanism: node A (bluish green #009E73), node B (orange #E69F00), node C (orange #E69F00), node D (light gray #999999, the "feedback vs. nothing" rival). Beneath each child node place a single small downward arrow ending in a short open-ended slot (blank) representing the distinguishing measurement that design would require. Keep all four branch angles symmetric. Use 1pt strokes, flat fills, no shadows, no gradients. Every node and slot must be blank and unannotated — no text or labels baked in. Components: one root node, four mechanism nodes, four branch lines, four distinguishing-measurement slots. White background, symmetric balanced layout, aligned node row.

### Block 2 — Full SCOPE Prompt
[S - Specification] single-column 89mm textbook, 300 DPI, vector, white, unannotated.
[C - Content] one root node (shared observation), four child nodes (competing mechanisms), four connecting branch lines, four downward distinguishing-measurement slots. Relationship to preserve: one result branches into four mutually exclusive mechanisms each needing a different measurement.
[O - Organization] root centered top, four children in a single aligned horizontal row below, symmetric branch lines, measurement slots directly beneath each child.
[P - Presentation] flat vector, Okabe-Ito: root #56B4E9, primary mechanism #009E73, two secondary #E69F00, neutral rival #999999; 1pt strokes; no text in image.
[E - Exclusions] no text, no node labels, no captions, no extra branch levels, no curved/organic tree foliage, no shadows, no gradients, no human figures, no 3D.

## Figure 4: Fluent-but-Hollow vs. Specific-and-Falsifiable
Priority: Supplementary
Trigger: VG — paired contrast of two claim types not visually obvious from prose
Figure type: Comparison panels (two-column paired contrast)
Concept statement: A fluent claim can accommodate any finding while a specific claim can be contradicted, so prose polish is not evidence of a testable claim.
Reader prior knowledge: Reader is primed to mistake polished prose for rigor; has not seen the two claim types juxtaposed by what each rules out.
Source anchor: Section: "a well-written introduction is evidence of a good research claim" / Popper test

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Place two equal vertical panels side by side inside a single-column textbook frame on white, divided by one thin vertical rule. Left panel header band light gray (#999999) = fluent but hollow; right panel header band bluish green (#009E73) = specific and falsifiable. Inside the left panel draw a single broad blank claim block with a wide arc beneath it sweeping to encircle several small scattered dots, representing a claim that accommodates every possible finding. Inside the right panel draw a single narrow blank claim block with one short straight arrow pointing down to a single open-ended slot (the failure condition), and beside it one small "X" mark in vermillion (#D55E00) indicating a finding that would defeat it. Keep panels symmetric in outer dimensions. Use 1pt strokes, flat fills, no shadows, no gradients. All blocks, dots, and slots must be blank and unannotated — no text baked in. Components: two header bands, two claim blocks, one accommodating arc with scattered dots, one failure-condition arrow + slot, one defeat marker. White background, aligned panel tops.

### Block 2 — Full SCOPE Prompt
[S - Specification] single-column 89mm textbook, 300 DPI, vector, white, unannotated.
[C - Content] left panel: broad claim block + wide accommodating arc enclosing scattered outcome dots; right panel: narrow claim block + single failure-condition arrow to a slot + one defeat "X". Relationship to preserve: hollow claim absorbs all outcomes, specific claim can be defeated by one.
[O - Organization] two equal columns split by a thin vertical rule, header bands aligned, contrast read left (hollow) to right (falsifiable).
[P - Presentation] flat vector, Okabe-Ito: gray #999999 (hollow), #009E73 (falsifiable), one #D55E00 defeat marker; 1pt strokes; no text in image.
[E - Exclusions] no text, no example sentences, no captions, no extra markers, no shading, no shadows, no gradients, no human figures, no 3D.
