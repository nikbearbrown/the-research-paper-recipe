## Figure 1: The construct-instrument gap
Priority: Critical
Trigger: VG — structural claim that an unobservable construct connects to an observable instrument only through an inference whose quality is "validity"
Figure type: Conceptual map
Concept statement: Validity is the quality of the inferential arrow from an unobservable construct to an observable instrument score, not a property of the instrument itself.
Reader prior knowledge: May think a "validated instrument" is valid in the abstract; needs to see validity located on the inference, not the tool.
Source anchor: Section: "There are two things in every measurement study"

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw two nodes side by side. On the left, a construct node drawn as an outlined-only circle in neutral gray with no fill, signaling it is unobservable. On the right, an instrument node drawn as a solid filled box in primary blue (#56B4E9), signaling it is observable. Connect them with a single dashed arrow pointing from the construct to the instrument, rendered in secondary orange (#E69F00) and clearly thicker than the node outlines, because this inferential arrow is the focus. Add a short bracket or caliper mark spanning the gap beneath the arrow to emphasize that the distance between construct and instrument is the object of interest. Keep two nodes, one dashed arrow, one gap bracket — four components. Single-headed arrow only, 1pt node strokes. Blank unannotated flat vector on white background, no text anywhere in the image.

## Figure 2: Reliability vs. validity
Priority: Critical
Trigger: VG — two orthogonal properties (consistency vs. on-target interpretation) best shown as the classic four-quadrant target comparison
Figure type: Comparison panels
Concept statement: Reliability is consistency of scores and validity is hitting the right target; an instrument can be reliable without being valid, but not the reverse for a single interpretation.
Reader prior knowledge: Knows the thermometer-reads-four-degrees-high example; the dartboard target metaphor makes the orthogonality concrete.
Source anchor: Section: "Reliability is related but distinct"

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw two concentric-ring target panels side by side, each a bullseye of three plain rings with a center dot. On the left panel, place a tight cluster of marks far from the center — reliable but not valid: consistent yet off-target. On the right panel, place a tight cluster of marks centered on the bullseye — reliable and valid. Render the cluster marks on the left in secondary orange (#E69F00) and the cluster marks on the right in green (#009E73) to signal the on-target success. Keep the ring outlines neutral gray, 1pt. Both panels identical in size and ring structure so only the cluster position differs. Two target panels, two clusters of four small marks each, one center dot per panel — six to eight components. Blank unannotated flat vector on white background, no text anywhere in the image.

## Figure 3: Retrieval strength vs. storage strength over time
Priority: Critical
Trigger: PQ — two forgetting curves over time whose ranking can reverse; a genuinely quantitative claim plotted on shared axes
Figure type: Statistical/quantitative
Concept statement: An immediate test measures retrieval strength and can rank conditions opposite to a delayed test, which measures durable storage strength, because the steeper-forgetting condition can start higher.
Reader prior knowledge: Understands forgetting happens over time; needs to see that immediate testing can invert the true durable ranking.
Source anchor: Section: "Fourth: what timescale does the measure support?"

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw a line chart with a horizontal time axis and a vertical performance axis, the vertical axis beginning at zero. Plot two smooth descending curves from left to right. Curve A starts high on the left and drops steeply, ending low on the right — high immediate, poor retention — drawn in secondary orange (#E69F00). Curve B starts lower on the left but declines shallowly, ending above Curve A on the right — lower immediate, better retention — drawn in primary blue (#0072B2). The two curves cross once, so that the left-edge ranking is reversed at the right edge. Mark two thin vertical reference lines, one at the immediate time point and one at a later delayed time point, to show where the ranking flips. Keep two curves, two axes, two reference lines — six components. 1pt strokes. Blank unannotated flat vector on white background, no text or numbers anywhere in the image.

## Figure 4: Instruction–assessment distance
Priority: Important
Trigger: VG — structural claim that valid learning measurement requires separation between the instruction experience and the test along several axes
Figure type: Comparison panels
Concept statement: A true learning claim requires distance between instruction and assessment — different format, different surface features, elapsed time — whereas aligned testing collapses that distance and confounds learning with test-specific preparation.
Reader prior knowledge: Has seen the aligned-quiz problem; needs to see "distance" as a measurable spatial gap between two regions.
Source anchor: Section: "Many studies measure learning with the same instrument"

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw two side-by-side panels each containing two boxes: an instruction box on the left and an assessment box on the right. In the upper panel, the instruction box and assessment box sit directly adjacent with almost no gap and identical shapes, both in secondary orange (#E69F00), depicting aligned, overlapping measurement. In the lower panel, the instruction box (orange #E69F00) and the assessment box (primary blue #0072B2) are clearly separated by a wide gap, drawn with different box shapes to signal different format and surface features. Place a short horizontal caliper or bracket in each panel spanning the gap between instruction and assessment to make the contrast in distance explicit. Two panels, four boxes, two gap brackets — eight components maximum. 1pt strokes. Blank unannotated flat vector on white background, no text anywhere in the image.
