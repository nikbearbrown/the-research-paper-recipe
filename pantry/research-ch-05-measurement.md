# Research: Chapter 05 — Measurement
## The Research Paper Recipe

**Chapter one-line:** Measurement — Construct, instrument, five questions, reading prior work
**Research date:** 2026-05-29

---

## 1. Primary Sources

### Foundational papers and texts
- **Samuel Messick, _Validity of psychological assessment: Validation of inferences from persons' responses and performances as scientific inquiry into score meaning_ (1995, American Psychologist).** Foundational for treating validity as an argument about inference, not a property stamped onto a test. Central to construct-measure gap.
- **Cronbach and Meehl, _Construct validity in psychological tests_ (1955, Psychological Bulletin).** Gives construct validity and nomological network as a way to reason about whether an instrument indexes the intended construct.
- **Robert A. Bjork and Elizabeth L. Bjork, _A new theory of disuse and an old theory of stimulus fluctuation_ (1992, chapter).** Supports the distinction between immediate performance and durable learning through storage and retrieval strength.
- **AERA, APA, and NCME, _Standards for Educational and Psychological Testing_ (2014, standards volume).** Gives current professional consensus for validity, reliability, fairness, and test interpretation.

### Key empirical cases
- **Immediate post-test vs delayed retention in learning studies.** Use as a recurring example: a measure can capture performance without capturing durable learning.
- **Self-report engagement surveys in educational technology.** Students may report satisfaction or time-on-task, but those proxies can diverge from learning or understanding.
- **Validated scale modification case.** A researcher changes items in an established scale; reliability and validity evidence no longer transfer automatically.

---

## 2. The Core Concept — State of the Field

### What is settled
Validity concerns the interpretation and use of scores. Reliability is necessary but not sufficient; a measure can be consistent and still measure the wrong construct. Claims should be scoped to the instrument, population, and timescale.

### What is disputed
Debates persist over reflective vs formative measurement, use of alpha as a default reliability index, and whether standardized instruments capture local or culturally specific constructs fairly.

### What has changed recently (last 5 years)
Learning analytics and AI platforms generate abundant behavioral traces, increasing proxy-measure temptation. Current measurement work stresses validity arguments and fairness across groups.

---

## 3. Application Domain Examples

- Write construct and instrument as separate lines in every Methods plan.
- Audit conclusions for claims broader than the measure.
- Require delayed or transfer measures when claiming learning.
- Use reliability and validity evidence before accepting composite scores.

---

## 4. The Book's Thesis Connection

The book's schema requires evidence to support the actual claim, not an adjacent measurable proxy. The student must supply construct judgment; AI can point out likely proxy gaps but cannot decide what the construct means in the discipline.

A self-directed student must bring their own disciplinary expertise to the examples: the relevant construct, the plausible comparison, the meaningful baseline, and the consequences of overclaiming. Tool output can help expose gaps, but it cannot certify that the research question matters or that the interpretation is warranted.

## 6. Pedagogical Delivery Research

Misconception: what is easy to measure is what matters. Teach with matched pairs of construct/instrument/conclusion and ask whether the conclusion exceeds the measure.

Effective delivery should use worked examples, contrastive cases, and error diagnosis. The target reader needs to see not only the correct form but the plausible wrong form: the vague topic, the causal overclaim, the hidden proxy, the p-value-only result, the source-by-source literature review, or the fluent but unsupported sentence.

## 10. AI Use Research — When To Use AI / When NOT To Use AI


**Series calibration:** This book belongs to *Irreducibly Human: What AI Can and Can't Do*. Across chapters, AI should be framed as a tool for interrogation, audit, transformation, and drafting from a completed specification. It should not be framed as the source of the student's research question, causal judgment, construct definition, evidentiary standard, or accountability. Current publication guidance also matters: ICMJE's 2026 recommendations state that AI tools should not be listed as authors and that humans remain responsible for accuracy, attribution, permissions, plagiarism checks, and transparent AI use. COPE and major publishers follow the same basic accountability principle.

### When to use AI
- Use AI to identify possible construct-measure gaps between what the paper claims and what the instrument observes.
- Use AI to suggest threats involving alignment, baseline, population, timescale, and transfer.
- Use AI to compare Methods and Discussion sections for conclusions that outrun the stated measure.

### When NOT to use AI
- Do not let AI define the construct for the field. Construct meaning is disciplinary and theoretical.
- Do not use AI to declare a measure valid without examining validation evidence, population, and use case.
- Do not let AI convert proxy measures such as clicks, satisfaction, or immediate scores into broad claims such as learning or understanding.

### Disclosure and accountability note
If AI helped audit construct-measure alignment, disclose it as measurement-claim review. The author should state which construct definition and validity evidence were used.

### Candidate classroom prompt
Here is my construct, instrument, population, and conclusion. Identify every place where the conclusion may exceed what this instrument can support.

### Research/policy anchors
- ICMJE Recommendations, updated January 2026: AI tools should not be authors; humans remain responsible for accuracy, attribution, permissions, plagiarism checks, and transparent AI use.
- COPE position statement on authorship and AI tools, 2023: AI tools cannot take responsibility for submitted work, so they cannot meet authorship requirements.
- Nature Portfolio AI policy family: generative AI use is policy-sensitive, developing rapidly, and must be disclosed or handled according to venue rules.
