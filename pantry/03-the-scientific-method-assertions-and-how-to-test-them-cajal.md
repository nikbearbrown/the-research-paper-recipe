## Figure 3: Null Hypothesis Test as Decision Procedure
Priority: Critical
Trigger: MC — Neyman-Pearson procedure is a multi-step decision flow (assume null → compute surprise → compare to threshold → decide)
Figure type: Process flowchart (decision procedure with branch)
Concept statement: Hypothesis testing is a decision procedure: assume the null, measure how surprising the data are under it, and at a pre-set threshold decide whether to act as if the null is false.
Reader prior knowledge: Reader thinks testing "finds out if the null is true"; has not seen it as a conditional decision rule with a branch.
Source anchor: Section: "Neyman and Pearson formalized statistical hypothesis testing... as a decision procedure"

### Block 1 — Illustrae Paste Block
Draw a top-to-bottom flowchart inside a single-column textbook frame on white. Box 1 at top (sky blue #56B4E9) = assume the null is true. A single downward arrow leads to box 2 (blue #0072B2) = compute how surprising the data are. A downward arrow leads to a diamond decision node (orange #E69F00) = is the surprise past the threshold. From the diamond, two single arrows branch: one going down-left to an end box (bluish green #009E73) = act as though the null is false (reject); one going down-right to an end box (light gray #999999) = do not reject. Keep the spine vertical and the two branch ends symmetric below the diamond. Use 1pt strokes, flat fills, no shadows, no gradients. All boxes and the diamond blank and unannotated — no text baked in. Components: two process boxes, one decision diamond, two outcome boxes, four arrows. White background, vertical spine, symmetric terminal branches.
